A modern JavaScript recreation of an original Flash-based interactive art piece. This 2026 version was restored and remade by **Gemini 3.1**, based on the seminal work of [**Hsin-Chien Huang**](https://hsinchienhuang.com/index.php?lang=ch) ([Wikipedia](https://zh.wikipedia.org/zh-tw/%E9%BB%83%E5%BF%83%E5%81%A5)).

#### Concept
The piece explores the recursive nature of time and space. A single square represents a moment or a whole, which can be split into smaller and smaller fragments. As it reaches its maximum complexity, it inevitably flows back into its original unified state.

#### Technical Implementation
Rebuilding this algorithmic experience required a combination of recursive data structures and precise physical simulation:

*   **Recursive Tree Architecture**: The logic uses a tree-based splitting algorithm. For each level of depth ($n$), the algorithm traverses the current "moment" and divides it into two fragments. To ensure organic irregularity, the split ratios are randomized between 20% and 80%, ensuring that every fragment maintains enough geometric "material" to support subsequent recursion steps.
*   **Coordinate Inheritance & Continuous Motion**: A primary challenge was achieving smooth, non-jumping motion. Every fragment inherits its parent’s exact coordinates and dimensions at the moment of fracture. As the square fragments, the children are initialized to perfectly overlap the parent before sliding out to their irregular targets, creating a seamless visual decomposition.
*   **"Ice Sliding" Physics**: To capture the original's physical weight, I implemented custom **Cubic-Bezier impulse curves** (`[0.05, 0.9, 0.1, 1.0]`). This simulates a frictionless surface—each split provides a sharp, high-velocity "push" followed by a long, slow glide as fragments settle into their new positions.
*   **Generative Web Audio Synthesis**: The chimes are synthesized in real-time using the **Web Audio API**. Each split triggers a complex tone consisting of a base frequency layered with multiple non-integer harmonics, creating a spacious, "glassy" resonance. The merging phase mirrors this by utilizing an exponential gain swell, effectively playing the chime in reverse.
*   **Atomic State Handover**: To eliminate visual jitter, the merge sequence uses an atomic handover mechanism. Fragments slide back to their parent's origin with surgical precision; only once they have perfectly aligned is the parent square restored, ensuring a flicker-free transition back to unity.
